Parameter Value Units Description Source
U1 2.66 × 10−1 M^-1sec^-1 Maximum uptake rate (Vmax) of eGlc This value was fitted from a known value of Ks (Dykhuizen, 1978) and data on E.coli growth dynamics (Jain & Srivistava, 2009)
M1 2.69 × 10−4 sec^-1 Rate of dGTP synthesis from iGlc This value was fitted to lab data on E.coli growth dynamics
Ks 3.97 × 10−5 M Concentration of glucose at which glucose uptake rate of ½ Vmax is achieved Ks measured as 7.16 ug/ml = 3.974e-5 M (Dykhuzien, 1978)
I1 6.90 × 10−3 sec^-1 Rate of dGTP incorporation into DNA opposite C Fitted to give known cytoplasmic concentration dGTP in exponential growth phase E coli of 92 uM in 0.4% glucose (Buckstein et al, 2008)
D1 6.90 × 10−3 sec^-1 Rate of C pairing opposite incorporated G Given the same value as I1 as the synthesis of new DNA (GDNA) and new genomes (Gcell) should generally proceed at equal rates (this is violated during rapid exponential growth (Bipatnath et al, 1998) but not included in this model)
O2 1.20 × 101 M^-1sec^-1 Rate of dGTP oxidation to odGTP by ROS Titrated to give a mutation rate of 1.94e-10 base pair substitutions per nucleotide in 0.2% glucose minimal media (Foster et al, 2015)
I2 2.53 × 10−4 sec^-1 Rate of odGTP incorporation into DNA opposite adenine The relative efficiency of oG binding to A compared to G binding to C (I1) is 7.7e-8 : 2.1e-6 (Maki & Sekiguchi, 1992) therefore I2 = I1 * (7.7e-8/2.1e-6)
D2 2.00 × 10−4 sec^-1 Rate of C pairing opposite incorporated oG causing an AT->CG mutation Rate of C pairing opposite an oG relative to C:G (I1 & D1) is 6e-8:2.1e-6 (Maki & Sekiguchi, 1992)
C1 2.80 sec^-1 Rate of odGTP hydrolysis to odGMP by nudix hydrolase enzyme MutT (NudA) Value taken from Kcat of mutT measured ex vivo (Xia et al, 2005)
C2 3.50 × 10−4 sec^-1 Rate of removal of adenine base incorporated opposite 8-oxodG in the genome by enzyme MutY Value taken from Kcat of mutY measured ex vivo as 0.021 min-1 = 3.5e-4 sec-1 (Lu et al, 1996)
R1 2.00 × 10−4 sec^-1 Rate of oG insertion into DNA opposite cytosine Relative incorporation efficiency opposite C of odGTP:dGTP is 0.029 (Maki & Sekiguchi, 1992) ∴ R1 = I1 * 0.029
S 2.58 × 10−2 sec^-1 Rate of removal of adenine base incorporated opposite 8-oxodG in the genome by enzyme MutS Fitted to known rate of mutation in MutS knockout of 40x wildtype (Kang et al, 2019)
r 1.73 × 101 sec^-1 Rate of ROS production from iGlc relative to dGTP production rate Titrated to a known H2O2 production rate of 14 uM/second in 0.2% glucose minimal media normalised to cell volume (Seaver and Imlay, 2001)
O3 5.60 × 101 sec^-1 Rate of ROS degradation through reactions other than dGTP oxidation Fitted to give a standing ROS concentration of 1.9E-7M, midpoint of known 1.3E-07 - 2.5E-07 Molar in LB (Gonzalez-Flecha & Demple, 1997)
R2 2.53 × 10−4 sec^-1 Rate of adenine pairing opposite incorporated oG The relative efficiency of oG binding to A compared to G binding to C (I1) is 7.7e-8 : 2.1e-6 (Maki & Sekiguchi, 1992) therefore R2 = I1 * (7.7e-8/2.1e-6)

Adding superscript

library(stringr)

gtp1%>%
  text_transform(
    locations = cells_body(
      columns = c(Units),
      rows = ParameterD$Units=="M^-1sec^-1"
    ),
    fn = function(x){
      M<-"M"
      sec<-"sec"
      glue::glue("{M}<sup>{-1}</sup>{sec}<sup>{-1}</sup>")
    }
  )%>%
  text_transform(
    locations = cells_body(
      columns = c(Units),
      rows = ParameterD$Units=="sec^-1"
    ),
    fn = function(x){
      sec<-"sec"
      glue::glue("{sec}<sup>{-1}</sup>")
    }
  )->gtp2
